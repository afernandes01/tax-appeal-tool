What to Do Next

Based on the analysis above, here is how to proceed with your appeal:

1. Review your options. Look at both the Uniformity and Overvaluation analyses above. You can file based on either or both arguments in a single appeal.

2. Select your best comparables. Pick 3 to 5 properties from the tables above that are most similar to yours in size, age, and features. The Board of Review will evaluate whether your chosen comparables are truly comparable, so prioritize properties that a reasonable person would agree are similar to yours.

3. File your appeal. You can file directly with the Cook County Assessor's Office during the open appeal window for your township, or with the Board of Review after the Assessor completes their review. Filing is free and can be done online:

File with the Assessor's Office File with the Board of Review

4. Gather your evidence. For your filing, you will need the PINs and addresses of your chosen comparable properties, their assessed values, and photos of the front of your property. For overvaluation, include sale prices and dates.

5. Check your deadlines. Appeal windows vary by township. Check the Board of Review schedule for your township's dates.
